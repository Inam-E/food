Here is how the popular Nigerian Jellof Rice(Naija jellof) is made.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups of long-grain white rice
  • 3 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 can of tomato paste
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons of thyme
  • 2 teaspoons of curry powder
  • 2 teaspoons of smoked paprika
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly and set it aside.
  2. In a blender or food processor, blend together the tomato paste, onion, red bell pepper, green bell pepper, and garlic until smooth.
  3. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the blended tomato mixture and cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the thyme, curry powder, smoked paprika, and salt to the pot and stir well.
  5. Add the rice to the pot and stir well to coat it with the tomato mixture.
  6. Add the chicken or vegetable broth to the pot and stir well.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot.
  8. Cook the rice for about 20-25 minutes, or until the liquid is absorbed and the rice is tender.
  9. Remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for about 5 minutes.
  10. Fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot.

FACTS

Jollof rice is a popular dish in West Africa, and it is believed to have originated in the Senegambia region, which includes present-day Senegal and The Gambia. However, it is also widely consumed in Nigeria and other neighboring countries, and each country has its own variations on the dish.

Nigerian Jollof Rice is typically served with fried plantains, grilled chicken, or other side dishes.
