INGREDIENTS

  • 2 pounds of beef or goat meat, cut into small pieces
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of minced ginger

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a small bowl, mix together the vegetable oil, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, lemon juice, salt, black pepper, ginger, and garlic to make a marinade.
  2. Place the meat in a large bowl and pour the marinade over it. Mix well to ensure that the meat is evenly coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight.
  3. Preheat a grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Remove the meat from the marinade and thread it onto skewers. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes before using to prevent them from burning.
  5. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 8-10 minutes on each side, or until the meat is browned and cooked to your liking.
  6. Serve the Nyama Choma hot with your favorite dipping sauce and side dishes
