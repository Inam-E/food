INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup teff flour
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups water

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the teff flour, all-purpose flour, and salt.
  2. Gradually whisk in the water until smooth.
  3. Cover with a towel and let sit at room temperature for 24-48 hours until bubbly and sour-smelling.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Pour about 1/4 cup of batter into the skillet and swirl it around to coat the bottom of the skillet.
  6. Cook until bubbles form on top of the injera and the edges start to curl up, about 2 minutes.
  7. Remove from skillet and repeat with remaining batter.

FACTS

According to the European Patent Office, Injera was invented by a Dutchman named Jans Roosjen in 20031. He is listed as the “inventor” of teff flour and associated food products1.

To store Injera, let it cool fully before storing it. Then, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap or foil and store it in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. If you want to freeze Injera, place it in an airtight container or plastic bag and store it in the freezer for up to 3 months.
